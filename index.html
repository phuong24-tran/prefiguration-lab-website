Prefiguration Lab

A research collective exploring how organizations can prefigure more sustainable, just futures.

We study practices, technologies, and forms of organizing that anticipate the world we want to build — from AI and surveillance to sustainability and social justice.

Our Team

The Prefiguration Lab brings together a professor and student researchers working on activism, technology, and more sustainable futures.

Prof. Mathieu Bouchard

Principal Investigator · Telfer School of Management

Mathieu Bouchard is an assistant professor of strategy and organization at the University of Ottawa's Telfer School of Management. He studies activism for sustainable policymaking out of a belief that if we want to create a better future, we need to act in the present.

He enjoys bebop jazz, his daughter's choreographies, and would like to learn chess but never finds the time.

Student Researchers

Adithiyan Rajan

Graduate Student Researcher

Adithiyan Rajan is a Computer Science graduate student who specializes in turning cutting-edge AI and Machine Learning research into practical, high-impact solutions.

His work spans deploying Large Language Models to strengthen cybersecurity workflows, building real-time detection systems, and developing applied intelligence tools that address complex, multidisciplinary challenges. At the University of Ottawa, he works with Prof. Mathieu Bouchard on research exploring how digital activism influences technology policy, applying AI and LLM-based methods to analyze public records and uncover insights into AI governance.

in

Phuong Tran

Student Researcher

Phuong Tran is a Business Technology Management student at the Telfer School of Management, exploring the intersection of emerging technologies, digital policy, and user-centric design.

She works as a research assistant on AI governance and tech policy, contributing to projects involving digital activism, data visualization, and regulatory analysis. Her interests span cybersecurity, UX storytelling, and public-sector innovation, and she is passionate about transforming complex problems into accessible, impactful solutions through research, design, and collaborative innovation.
